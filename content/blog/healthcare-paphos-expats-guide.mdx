Moving to a new country comes with one big anxiety: *"What happens if I get sick?"*. If you've just arrived in Paphos, you've probably heard mixed opinions about healthcare. The reality on the ground is more reassuring: **healthcare in Cyprus-and especially in Paphos-is fast, practical, and much easier to navigate than many expats expect.**

Unlike markets where public care often means long queues, Cyprus tends to move faster.

## The Myth of the Waiting List

One of the biggest surprises for expats in Paphos is the speed of **GESY (General Healthcare System)** access. In places like the UK or Spain, waiting months for a specialist can be normal. In Cyprus, timelines are usually much shorter.

In practice, the public system performs very well. For most specialties, waiting lists are limited. Many patients secure appointments within the **same week, or at most the same month**. Whether you need a GP (Personal Doctor) or a specialist, the classic "waiting game" is far less common than many newcomers assume.

## How the System Works: GESY vs. Private

### 1. GESY (The Public System)
Once you have your yellow slip and are contributing to Social Insurance, you can register for GESY.
- **The GP (Personal Doctor):** Your gateway to the system. You can usually see them within 24-48 hours.
- **Specialists:** Your GP gives you a referral (*parapemptiko*), and you can book a specialist for a small co-payment (usually around EUR6).

### 2. Private Healthcare
Many expats still keep private insurance or pay out-of-pocket for selected services. Why? It can give access to specific doctors outside the GESY network and, in some cases, even faster diagnostics.
